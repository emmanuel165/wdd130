Nigeria

Nigeria, officially the Federal Republic of Nigeria, is located in West Africa, bordered by the Sahel to the north and the Gulf of Guinea to the south. With an area of 923,769 square kilometers and a population exceeding 230 million, it is Africa's most populous country and the world's sixth-largest. Nigeria's history spans indigenous states and kingdoms before British colonialization in the 19th century, leading to its independence in 1960. The country has a diverse ethnic landscape with over 250 groups, prominent among them being the Hausa, Yoruba, and Igbo. English is the official language, and Nigeria boasts significant Muslim and Christian populations, contributing to its religious diversity. Economically, Nigeria ranks as Africa's second-largest economy, plays a pivotal role in regional affairs, and holds membership in global organizations such as the UN, AU, and OPEC.
